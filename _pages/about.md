I am a PhD student at [Sorbonne Université](https://www.sorbonne-universite.fr/), working in the [Aramis Lab](https://www.aramislab.fr/) at the [Paris Brain Institute](https://institutducerveau-icm.org/) (ICM). My supervisors are [Ninon Burgos](https://ninonburgos.com/) and [Olivier Colliot](https://oliviercolliot.github.io/).

My thesis focuses on **longitudinal processing of neuroimages for the study of neurodegenerative diseases**, with an emphasis on MRI registration, segmentation, and benchmarking.

Before starting my PhD (March 2024), I worked for nearly three years as a **medical imaging research engineer** in the same lab, contributing to the development of [Clinica](https://www.clinica.run/), an open-source neuroimaging software platform. I worked on standardising datasets to the [BIDS](https://bids.neuroimaging.io/) format and refactoring image processing pipelines using [Nipype](https://nipype.readthedocs.io/) and [Pydra](https://github.com/nipype/pydra).

I hold a *Diplôme d'ingénieur* from [Telecom Physique Strasbourg](https://www.telecom-physique.fr/) (2020).

## Research interests

- Longitudinal MRI processing and benchmarking
- Brain registration and segmentation
- Reproducible neuroimaging workflows
- Open-source software for clinical research
